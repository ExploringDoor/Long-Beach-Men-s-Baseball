⚙️ Manager Admin Guide
Long Beach Diamond Classics — How to Enter Box Scores
🔒 This document is for Team Captains/Managers only

🌐 long-beach-men-s-baseball.vercel.app → Admin

As a team manager, you are responsible for entering your team's box score after each game. This keeps standings, stats, and player records up to date for the entire league. Please try to enter your box score on the same day as the game, while it's fresh in your memory.

This guide walks you through every step — very slowly and clearly. If you ever get confused, just start over from Step 1 and follow along. It takes about 5–10 minutes once you get the hang of it.

⚠️ Before you start — things to have ready: Your team's lineup card from the game · Each player's stats (AB, R, H, RBI, BB, K) · Pitcher's innings pitched and stats · The final score
1
Go to the Admin Page
🌐

Open the website

On your phone or computer, go to: long-beach-men-s-baseball.vercel.app

👆

Click "ADMIN" in the menu

Look at the navigation bar across the top of the page. You'll see: HOME · SCORES · SCHEDULE · STANDINGS · TEAMS · STATS · HISTORY · RULES · ADMIN

Click on ADMIN — it's the last item in the menu.

🔑

You will see a login screen

There are two login options. You want the one at the bottom called "CAPTAIN LOGIN."

Do NOT use "Admin Login" — that is for the league commissioner only.

2
Log In as Your Team
⬇️

Scroll down to "Captain Login"

Under the Captain Login section, you will see a dropdown menu that says "— Select your team —"

🖱️

Tap the dropdown and select YOUR team

Tap the dropdown menu. A list of all 7 teams will appear. Tap your own team name. For example, if you manage Brooklyn, tap "Brooklyn."

🟢

Tap the green button

After selecting your team, a green button will appear that says "LOG IN AS [YOUR TEAM]" — tap it!

Example: if you chose Brooklyn it will say "LOG IN AS BROOKLYN"

✅ You're in! You'll see a green bar at the top of the screen that says "Captain Portal — [Your Team]." This means you're logged in and ready to enter a box score.
3
Pick the Game You Played
📋

You'll see a list of your team's games

The screen shows all your team's scheduled games for the season — the opponent, date, time, and location.

Tap "Select →" next to today's game

Find the game you just played and tap the blue "Select →" button next to it. This loads the box score entry form for that specific game.

📌 Note about the tabs at the top: You'll see three buttons — "FROM SCHEDULE," "EDIT SAVED GAME," and "CUSTOM GAME."

· FROM SCHEDULE = normal — use this every week to enter a new game
· EDIT SAVED GAME = use this if you already submitted a game and need to fix a mistake
· CUSTOM GAME = only for games not on the schedule (rare)
4

At the top of the form there are two large boxes — one for each team. Each box currently shows "0." Tap each box and type in the final score.

Headline (optional)

There's a text field for a "Headline." This is optional — but it's fun! Type something like "Brooklyn Walks Off in the 9th!" or "Pirates Shut Out Titans 7-0." This headline shows up on the score card for everyone to see.

Next to the headline is a Status dropdown. It should say "Final." Leave it as "Final" for a completed game. Only change it to "PPD" if the game was rained out/postponed.

5
Section 2 — Linescore (Optional)
📊

What is a linescore?

The linescore is the inning-by-inning score — like what you see on a real scoreboard. It shows how many runs each team scored in each inning (1 through 9), plus total Runs (R), Hits (H), and Errors (E).

✍️

How to fill it in

You'll see a grid with innings 1–9. Tap each small box and type the runs scored in that inning. Most boxes will be 0. The R column fills in automatically when you enter the score in Step 4.

💡 This section is optional. If you don't have the inning-by-inning breakdown, you can skip the linescore boxes and just leave them at 0. The final score from Step 4 is what matters most.
6
Section 3 — Batting Lineups
⚾

Two options: Score Only or Full Stats

For each team there are two tabs at the top of the batting section: "SCORE ONLY" and "FULL STATS."

· Score Only = just track who played, no individual stats needed (fast!)
· Full Stats = enter every player's AB, R, H, RBI, BB, K (more detail, takes longer)

✅ Recommendation: Use Full Stats — it's what makes the Stats page and player leaderboards work! Once you set the batting order it's actually very quick to fill in.
1️⃣

Click "FULL STATS" — Set Batting Order appears immediately

As soon as you tap "FULL STATS," the screen switches to the Set Batting Order screen. You will see a blue panel that says:

Set Batting Order — Tap each player in the order they bat

Below that, you'll see all your players shown as cards.

Set Batting Order
Tap each player in the order they bat — #1 first, then #2, and so on. Tap a player again to remove them.

👆

Tap players in batting order — #1 first, then #2, etc.

Simply tap the player who batted first in your lineup. A green "1" will appear on their card. Then tap the player who batted second — a green "2" will appear. Keep going until all players are numbered.

↩️

Made a mistake? Tap them again!

If you tapped the wrong player, just tap them again and their number disappears — then tap the correct player. You can also use the "↩ Undo" button to undo the last tap, or "Cancel" to start over.

✅

Order saves automatically!

Once you've tapped every player, the order saves automatically and you're taken back to the batting stats screen. The players are now listed in batting order.

📊

Enter each player's stats using the + / − buttons

For each player you'll see their stats: AB · R · H · RBI · BB · K · 2B · 3B · HR · SB

Each stat has a minus (−) button on the left and a plus (+) button on the right. Just tap + to add 1, or − to subtract 1. No typing needed!

🔴

Toggle off players who didn't play

Each player has a small green toggle switch on the right side. If a player on your roster did NOT play in this game, tap their toggle to switch it off (it turns gray). That removes them from the stats for this game.

💡 What do the stats mean?
AB = At Bats · R = Runs Scored · H = Hits · RBI = Runs Batted In · BB = Walks · K = Strikeouts · 2B = Doubles · 3B = Triples · HR = Home Runs · SB = Stolen Bases
📌 Important — Do both teams! The batting section has two columns — one for the away team and one for the home team. You need to fill in stats for both teams, not just your own. Get the other team's lineup card if you can!
7
Section 4 — Pitching
⚾

Enter each pitcher's name and stats

For each pitcher, you'll see a text box for their name and stat fields: IP, H, R, ER, BB, K, HR and a Decision dropdown (W/L/S/ND).

Type the pitcher's name in the box. Then use the + and − buttons to enter their stats.

📝

Innings Pitched (IP)

IP uses decimals: 6.0 = 6 full innings · 6.1 = 6 innings and 1 out · 6.2 = 6 innings and 2 outs. Just type the number directly in the IP box.

🏆

Select the Decision

Use the small Decision dropdown (it says "ND" by default) to mark the pitcher's result:
· W = Win · L = Loss · S = Save · ND = No Decision (relief pitcher with no result)

➕

Multiple pitchers? Tap "+ Add Pitcher"

If more than one pitcher threw in the game, tap the "+ Add Pitcher" button to add another row. Fill in each pitcher separately.

8
Section 5 — Game Recap (Optional)
✍️

Write a recap (optional but fun!)

There's a text box where you can write a brief description of the game — anything from 1 sentence to a few lines. This shows up when people click "RECAP" on the score card.

Example: "Brooklyn rallied for 4 runs in the 7th to defeat the Pirates 8-5. Craig McKendall went 3-for-4 with 2 RBI to lead the offense."

If you leave this blank, a simple auto-generated recap will be created from the stats.

9
Submit the Box Score!
⬇️

Scroll to the bottom of the page

After filling in all sections, scroll all the way down to the bottom of the form.

🏟️ Submit Box Score

You'll see a large green button that says "Submit Box Score." Tap it once. The page will show a confirmation message and the game will be saved to the website immediately — standings, stats, and leaderboards all update right away!

✅ SUBMIT BOX SCORE
🎉 Done! The box score is now live on the website. Everyone can see the score, box score, and stats instantly. Great job!
⚠️ Double-check before submitting! Make sure the final score is correct, and that the winning team's score is higher. Once submitted, a mistake can be fixed using "Edit Saved Game" — but it's easier to get it right the first time.

🔧 How to Edit a Game You Already Submitted

Made a mistake? No problem! Here's how to fix it:


1. Go to Admin → Log in as your team (same as always)
2. At the top of the form, tap the "✏️ EDIT SAVED GAME" button
3. A list of games you've already submitted will appear — find the one you need to fix and tap "Edit →"
4. The form opens pre-filled with the existing data — just change whatever is wrong
5. Scroll down and tap "Update Box Score" to save your changes
